Rule-Based Sentiment Analysis
-uses a set of predefined rules that connects words to emotions or attitudes.
- Rule based sentiment analysis cannot handle more subtle expressions, such as sarcasm or irony

******TextBlob and VADER is used for rule based sentiment analysis as python packages*******

- A lexicon: a collection of words paired with their predefined sentiment scores

- because of their different lexicons, TextBlob and VADER may produce different results 
when analyzing the same text 

-it is good to test one more than one package on a sample of your text
as different packages may give different results of positivity or negativity
- comparing their outputs helps you decide which one works best for your case 


-Rule based sentiment analysis is easy to understand but not always accurate



-Transformers can capture how words influence and relate to each other 
even when they far apart in a sentence 
-Transformers can understand context 
- pipeline() function: provides a quick way to use powerful pretrained models
without needing to build them from scratch

**to improve the results you can use a different pre-trained model
